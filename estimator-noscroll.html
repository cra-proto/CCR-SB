Canada Carbon Rebate for Small Businesses

  • Who is eligible
  • How much you may get
  • How and when you will get the rebate

How much you may get

As an eligible CCPC, your total rebate amount will be equal to the number of employees you had in the designated provinces between 2019 and 2023 inclusively, multiplied by a payment rate for each of those provinces and years.

Estimate your payment

Use this tool to estimate your amount based on your business operations between 2019 and 2023. You can also refer to the payment rate tables to manually calculate your payment by referring to:

  • Rate tables for designated provinces
  • How employees are considered and counted

You must be eligible to receive the rebate before you can complete the estimator.

Read the privacy statement for the estimator

The CRA is not collecting or retaining any of the information you enter in this tool. These amounts are only used to help you estimate the Canada Carbon Rebate payment amount you may be eligible for.

Eligible years to claim
What is a eligible fuel charge year?

Your rebate is a single amount that includes all past years you were eligible for.

The years correspond to the calendar year in which a fuel charge year begins. A fuel charge year runs from April 1 to March 31.

You cannot claim the rebate for a certain year

You cannot claim a rebate for the:

  • 2023 fuel year
  • 2022 fuel year
  • 2021 fuel year
  • 2020 fuel year
  • 2019 fuel year

You have 500 or more employees working in Canada for that period.

Rate tables for designated provinces

Each province has a set rebate amount per employee for each year they were designated.

British Columbia, Northwest Territories, Nunavut, Quebec, and Yukon are not designated to receive the rebate from 2019 to 2023.

How employees are considered and counted

For the purposes of this rebate, an employee means a person who was both:

  • Employed by your corporation at any time in the calendar year
  • Issued a T4 statement of remuneration paid by your company

There is no distinction made between full-time, part-time, or seasonal employees.

An employee’s province of employment is the province or territory their T4 was issued for that year (box 10).

If you issued more than one T4 for an employee in different provinces or territories in the same year, include them only once, in the province or territory which their remuneration (box 14) was highest.

For more information on how a province of employment is determined, refer to: Determine the province of employment.
